Tribute to Ratan Tata

(1937-2024)

Industrialist | Philanthropist | Visionary Leader

"If you want to walk fast, walk alone. But if you want to walk far, walk together''

Biography

    Born in 1937, he is an heir of the Tata family, and son of Naval Tata who was later adopted by Ratanji Tata, son of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of Tata Group. He is an alumnus of the Cornell University College of Architecture and Harvard Business School through the Advanced Management Program that he completed in 1975. Ratan Tata is renowned for his philanthropic activities, primarily through Tata Trusts, which he guided for many years. His work has focused on various social issues, including healthcare, education, rural development, disaster relief, and women's empowerment. Tata Trusts have supported initiatives like maternal and child health, mental health, and the treatment of diseases like cancer, malaria, and tuberculosis.
